\section{Action and Conjugation}

\subsection{Action}

\defn{symmetric group, $S_n$}
\defn{
A group G \term{acts} on $\Omega$, if to every pair $(\alpha,g)\in\Omega\times G$, an element $\alpha^g\in\Omega$ is assigned s.t.\begin{enumerate}[i.]
\item{$\alpha^1=\alpha$ for $1=1_G$ and all $\alpha\in\Omega$,}
\item{$(\alpha^x)^y=\alpha^{xy}$ for all $x,y\in G$ and all $\alpha\in\Omega$.}
\end{enumerate}}

The mapping\\
\centerline{
$g^\pi:\Omega\to\Omega$ with $\alpha\mapsto\alpha^g$}
describes the action of $g\in G$ on $\Omega$. In particular $g^\pi$ is a bijection and thus a permutation on $\Omega$. \\

Now \romannumeral2. implies that\\
\centerline{
$\pi:G\to S_\Omega$ with $g\mapsto g^\pi$}
is a homomorphism.
